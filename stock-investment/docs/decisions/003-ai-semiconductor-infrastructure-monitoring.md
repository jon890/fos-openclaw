# ADR-003: AI semiconductor and infrastructure morning monitoring

- Status: Accepted
- Date: 2026-05-07

## Context

The user wants the daily morning investment brief to also track semiconductors because the AI market increasingly looks like a semiconductor/infrastructure competition. The existing brief already tracks CRCL, BTC, Google/Alphabet, and Nasdaq/QQQ.

## Decision

1. Add an `AI 반도체/인프라` first-class section to the morning brief.
2. Use `SMH` as the sector-level semiconductor proxy.
3. Track representative AI semiconductor/infrastructure names:
   - `NVDA` for accelerators/GPU/data-center AI demand
   - `TSM` for foundry, advanced packaging, and AI/HPC capacity
   - `AVGO` for AI ASIC/networking plus infrastructure software
   - `AMD` for AI GPU and data-center competition
   - `ASML` for EUV/High-NA and semiconductor CapEx cycle
   - `VRT` for AI data-center power/cooling infrastructure
4. Add relevant official/industry sources for NVIDIA, TSMC, ASML, Broadcom, AMD, SIA, and Vertiv.
5. Keep the section compact: summarize the basket and mention only the 2-3 most important individual moves or signals each morning.

## Guardrails

- Do not frame semiconductor momentum as automatic upside; explicitly check valuation/overheating and profit-taking risk.
- Distinguish AI demand narrative from confirmed revenue, orders, CapEx, and guidance.
- Include infrastructure bottlenecks such as advanced packaging, EUV, power, and cooling where evidence appears.
- Treat geopolitical/export-control risk as a standing risk for semiconductors.
